Charlie's Window Time

Years ago, I went to the local shelter and saw a dog way in the back isolated from the others. He was a mini pin Chihuahua mix with a gray mohawk. He stood in his tiny cage growling like a gremlin, his fur ruffled up and unkempt. I fell in love instantly despite the hesitation of the staff and my family who accompanied me. I requested to hold him. After some pause from a shelter worker, he was put in my arms. Our bond was immediate. He became my soul dog and constant, companion for years after until he passed from a cancerous tumor. This art piece is based off his love of his window time.
